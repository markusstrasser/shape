The Shape Fellowship


Sunday, February 6, 2022
The Shape Foundation is launching a fellowship to support exceptional upcoming engineers, researchers and founders in the biological and computer sciences.

The upcoming inaugural cohort of fellows will be invited to San Francisco for a full 2+ week program of get togethers, lab tours, journal clubs, group dinners, hikes, daytrips and meetups with leading deep tech researchers, engineers and founders. In short: phenomenal vibes.

The exact dates and duration are still TBA but will likely be either before or after the 2022 summer break so as to not clash with the usual dates for summer internships.

In spirit, the fellowship is similar to the Emergent Ventures fellowship and the NewScience summer program but with a decided focus on open source infrastructure, simulations and research software.


MOTIVATION
We created the Shape fellowship because many potentially valuable research applications and tools are malnurished. The academic incentives to maintain, host and make research software work for more than N=1 users don't exist. Most projects by themselves are not venture fundable and even if they were, creators most often don't want to create a company or GTM strategy around them.

We've seen this over and over again: novel and potentially crucial building blocks for future scientific advances are neglected. There's a large spread of (mostly very old) tools that everything relies on for basic tasks (ie. sequence alignment) and which are very hard to run and use. Half of bioinformatics is installing software tools correctly and the rest is just converting between file formats ... as the joke goes. All of this makes the barrier to entry and experimentation prohibitively high.


WHAT IS THE APPLICATION PROCESS?
We do two 30 minute video calls and one technical/brainstorming call to see if a potential fellow is a fit. There's currently no application form to fill out. You're here because of a referral or because we contacted you directly.

FUNDING
So far we have $1M in contributions predominantly from funders in the Bay Area and NYC who we'll share a few weeks from now. We will officially publicize the fellowship in winter (2022) after the second cohort has finished their stay in San Francisco. Please do not share this URL until then.
